4

Guardrails and red flags

Help reps stay accurate when discussing pricing, outcomes, and anything that belongs to clinicians instead of sales.[file:69][file:71]

+

What not to overstate

  • Do not present legacy pricing mock-ups as final unless production confirms them, because the PDF reference is explicitly inaccurate and template-like.[file:69]
  • Do not blur the line between program coordination and medical decision-making; clinical decisions are attributed to licensed providers.[file:71]
  • Do not promise outcomes beyond the source claims; keep the conversation on coordinated care, tracking, and muscle-preservation support positioning.[file:71]

Suggested safe phrasing

Say that pricing, medication selection, and clinical eligibility are subject to final program configuration and provider review, while the sales conversation should focus on the user experience and differentiated care model.[file:69][file:71]

Objections & FAQ

What if the prospect asks whether Vitelize itself provides medical care?

Use the disclaimer language: clinical services, including prescribing and monitoring, are provided by clinicians affiliated with CareValidate, while Vitelize provides coaching and program coordination.[file:71]

What if they ask why CoreProgression matters?

Explain that CoreProgression signals existing member status and supports training continuity, which fits the broader value proposition of preserving muscle during GLP-1-guided weight loss.[file:71][file:69]

What if they focus only on medication?

Reframe the conversation around the combined experience: intake, provider oversight, reminders, symptom logging, body-composition tracking, and fitness support, rather than medication alone.[file:71]

What if they question the screenshots with pricing?

Label those screens as reference visuals only until final production pricing and checkout logic are confirmed, because the provided mock-up is not reliable as a live source of truth.[file:69]
